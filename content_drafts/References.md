## 1. Global Basel / BIS Sources

### 1.1 Basel Framework – Big Picture

**1. Basel III: international regulatory framework for banks (overview page)**

**What:** Main Basel III landing page on the BIS site. High-level explanation plus links to capital, leverage, liquidity, market risk, large exposures, etc.

**Modules:** 1, 2, 3, 8, 11, 12, 13.

**How to use (beginner):**

* Start here if you’re ever lost about *where* a rule lives.
* Scroll to see sections for capital, leverage, liquidity, macro-prudential tools, Pillar 3, etc.

**Link:** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/basel3.htm`

---

**2. Background on the Basel Framework**

**What:** Short explainer on what the “Basel Framework” is and why BIS created the consolidated online rulebook.

**Modules:** 1, 2, 13.

**How to use:**

* 5-minute read to explain Basel’s role in an interview.
* Clarifies that the online Basel Framework is the *current* rulebook (not just legacy PDFs).

**Link:** `https://www.bis.org/baselframework/background.htm`

---

### 1.2 Core Capital Framework – Basel II & Basel III

**3. Basel II – International Convergence of Capital Measurement and Capital Standards: A Revised Framework (Comprehensive Version, BCBS 128)**

**What:** Full Basel II “big book” – original minimum capital rules, IRB formulas, Pillar 2 and Pillar 3.

**Modules:** 2, 4, 7, 8, 11, 12.

**How to use:**

* Treat it as the **maths / theory bible** behind IRB, EL/UL, Pillar 2 & Pillar 3 wording.
* Search inside the PDF for “IRB”, “PD”, “LGD”, “Pillar 2”, “Pillar 3”.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/publ/bcbs128.htm`

---

**4. Basel III: A global regulatory framework for more resilient banks and banking systems (BCBS 189)**

**What:** Original Basel III core capital reform text – defines CET1/AT1/Tier 2, buffers, leverage ratio, and key counterparty credit risk changes.

**Modules:** 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8, 11, 12.

**How to use:**

* Read the executive summary + early chapters to deeply understand **capital quality** (what counts as CET1/AT1/Tier 2).
* Use tables/paragraphs for exact capital component definitions and buffer language.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/publ/bcbs189.htm`

---

**5. Basel III: Finalising post-crisis reforms (Basel 3.1 / “Endgame”, BCBS 424)**

**What:** Basel 3.1 package – revises SA for credit risk, constrains IRB, introduces the **output floor (72.5%)**, and reforms op-risk and market-risk frameworks.

**Modules:** 2, 4, 8, 11, 12, 13.

**How to use:**

* Main reference for **output floor** and Basel 3.1 / endgame.
* Search for “output floor”, “internal ratings-based approaches”, “credit risk standardised approach”.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d424.htm`

---

### 1.3 Standardised Approach, CRM, Securitisation, Market & Op Risk

**6. Revisions to the Standardised Approach for Credit Risk (consultative, pre-Basel 3.1)**

**What:** Consultation papers explaining the design of the revised SA for credit risk (risk weights by LTV, rating bucket, product).

**Modules:** 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 12.

**How to use:**

* Great for understanding the **logic** behind new SA risk weights, even if final numbers moved.
* Focus on tables by exposure class: sovereign, bank, corporate, retail, real estate.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d307.htm`

---

**7. Revisions to the Securitisation Framework (BCBS 303 + related docs)**

**What:** Basel III overhaul of securitisation capital – SEC-IRBA/ERBA/SA hierarchy and the **1250% risk-weight fallback** for some exposures.

**Modules:** 8, 12.

**How to use:**

* Use to understand why some tranches are effectively **capital deductions** (1250% RW ≈ 100% capital charge).
* For our course, focus on the hierarchy and 1250% concept, not the full formulas.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d303.htm`

---

**8. Minimum capital requirements for market risk (FRTB final standard, BCBS 457)**

**What:** Final FRTB text – trading book boundary, Standardised Approach (SA-MR), and Internal Models Approach (IMA) for market risk.

**Modules:** 8, 12.

**How to use:**

* Read the intro + overview of SA-MR to be able to explain FRTB in interviews.
* Don’t worry about every formula; focus on the **conceptual shift** (Expected Shortfall, risk factor sensitivities).

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d457.htm`

---

**9. Standardised Measurement Approach (SMA) for Operational Risk**

**What:** Consultative docs and monitoring instructions that introduce the **SMA**, replacing AMA and unifying BIA/TSA into one approach.

**Modules:** 8, 12.

**How to use:**

* Focus on:

  * **Business Indicator (BI)**, and
  * **Loss Component / Internal Loss Multiplier (ILM)**.

* Enough to explain SMA conceptually in interviews.

**Useful entry point:** Basel III QIS / monitoring instructions that summarise SMA:
`https://www.bis.org/bcbs/qis/`

---

**10. Interest Rate Risk in the Banking Book (IRRBB, BCBS 368)**

**What:** Pillar 2 standard for IRRBB – how supervisors expect banks to measure and manage interest-rate risk in the **banking book**.

**Modules:** 2, 8, 11, 12.

**How to use:**

* Read early sections that define IRRBB and **standardised outlier tests**.
* Useful when talking about risks beyond simple credit/market/operational RWAs.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d368.htm`

---

### 1.4 Pillar 3 – Disclosure Framework

**11. Revised Pillar 3 disclosure requirements (Phase I, BCBS 309)**

**What:** 2015 standard that revamped Pillar 3 – introduces fixed templates/tables for capital, RWA, leverage, liquidity, etc.

**Modules:** 2, 10, 13.

**How to use:**

* Look at the templates to see what supervisors expect banks to publish.
* Inspires how you design your own “Pillar-3 style” summary screens.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d309.htm`

---

**12. Pillar 3 disclosure requirements – consolidated and enhanced framework (Phase II, BCBS 400)**

**What:** 2017 consolidation of Pillar 3 – integrates more risk types and detailed tables, building on BCBS 309.

**Modules:** 2, 8, 10, 13.

**How to use:**

* Reference point for table names and structure (capital, RWA by risk type, credit risk, CRM, securitisation, etc.).
* Good when mapping which RWA engine outputs feed which disclosure tables.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d400.htm`

---

**13. Pillar 3 disclosure requirements – updated framework (Phase III, BCBS 455)**

**What:** 2018 update – integrates Basel 3.1 changes into Pillar 3 templates (revised SA/IRB, market risk, operational risk).

**Modules:** 10, 12, 13.

**How to use:**

* If you want to be very up to date, skim how **new endgame metrics** flow into public disclosures.

**Link (info):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d455.htm`

---

### 1.5 Monitoring & “Meta” Documents

**14. Basel III Monitoring Reports (regular QIS / monitoring publications)**

**What:** Periodic BIS reports showing how banks’ capital, leverage and liquidity look under Basel III / 3.1, including estimated impact of reforms.

**Modules:** 1, 11, 12, 13.

**How to use:**

* Skim latest report to quote real-world numbers on:

  * Typical capital ratios,
  * RWA impact of Basel 3.1,
  * How often output floor binds, etc.

**Link (overview):** `https://www.bis.org/bcbs/qis/`

## 2. India / RBI Sources

*(Always go via RBI’s own site and master-circular/master-direction indices to get the latest versions.)*

### 2.1 Capital Adequacy – Basel III in India

**15. Master Circular – Basel III Capital Regulations (RBI)**

**What:** Consolidated prudential guidelines on capital adequacy for scheduled commercial banks – minimum ratios, capital components, buffers, leverage ratio, references to risk-weight rules, and disclosure pointers.

**Modules:** 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 10, 11, 12.

**How to use:**

* Bookmark RBI’s **Master Circulars index** and always download the most recent “Basel III Capital Regulations” circular.
* For every capital-ratio statement in your notes (9%, 11.5% with CCB, etc.), tie back to this document.

**Index:** `https://www.rbi.org.in/commonman/English/Scripts/MasterCircular.aspx`

(Recent bank Pillar-3 reports explicitly reference the latest Basel III master circular number and date – useful cross-check.)

---

**16. Basel II / NCAF – Prudential Guidelines on Capital Adequacy and Market Discipline – New Capital Adequacy Framework (NCAF)**

**What:** Indian Basel II implementation for credit risk (SA/IRB options), market and operational risk, plus Pillar 2 and Pillar 3 guidance.

**Modules:** 2, 4, 5, 8, 10, 12.

**How to use:**

* Good for seeing **exact RBI wording** on SA exposure classes, ECAIs, risk weights, and early disclosure formats.
* Still relevant because Basel III capital regulations often **cross-refer** to these SA details.

---

### 2.2 Asset Quality, NPAs & Provisioning

**17. Prudential Norms on Income Recognition, Asset Classification and Provisioning pertaining to Advances (IRACP / IRAC norms)**

**What:** Master Direction/Master Circular that defines NPA classification, restructuring norms, provisioning percentages by bucket, etc.

**Modules:** 5, 6, 8, 10, 11.

**How to use:**

* Use to align your **NPA buckets and provisioning** with RBI definitions.
* Helpful when linking:

  * Provisioning levels → NPA risk weights in Basel III capital rules, and
  * Provisioning vs EL (for IRB discussions).

---

### 2.3 Financial Statements & Notes to Accounts

**18. Master Direction – Financial Statements – Presentation and Disclosures (RBI/DOR/2021-22/83)**

**What:** Current master direction on how banks must present financial statements and notes – includes capital adequacy, NPAs, sectoral and concentration disclosures, etc.

**Modules:** 1, 3, 5, 10, 11.

**How to use:**

* This is your **template** when designing reporting views in your app – especially Notes to Accounts.
* Cross-link with:

  * Basel III Capital Regulations (for ratios/RWA), and
  * IRAC norms (for NPA/provision disclosures).

---

**19. Master Circular – Disclosure in Financial Statements – Notes to Accounts (older versions)**

**What:** Earlier RBI master circulars on Notes to Accounts; many tables in current practice still follow this style.

**Modules:** 1, 10.

**How to use:**

* Great learning tool: lists specific disclosure tables (capital adequacy, asset quality, sectoral exposure, concentration).
* Many Indian Pillar-3 and annual report tables are clearly descendants of these formats.

---

### 2.4 Pillar 3 Disclosures & Basel III Reporting in India

RBI’s Pillar 3 requirements are spread across:

* Basel III Capital Regulations (DF-tables),
* Legacy NCAF Pillar-3 sections,
* Financial Statements Master Direction / Notes to Accounts.

For practical learning, it’s best to look at **actual Indian Pillar-3/Basel-III disclosure PDFs** that implement DF-tables.

---

**20. Example – Basel III / Pillar 3 Disclosures (DF tables)**

**What:** Basel III disclosure reports from Indian entities (e.g., NHB, NABARD, private banks, foreign bank branches in India) that explicitly follow RBI DF-1, DF-2, DF-3, DF-4, DF-5, etc.

**Modules:** 4, 5, 7, 8, 10, 13.

**How to use:**

* Treat them as **worked examples** of:

  * DF-2: Capital adequacy,
  * DF-3/DF-4: Credit risk under SA,
  * DF-5: CRM,
  * DF-6–8: Securitisation, market risk, operational risk.

* When the curriculum says “DF-4 – Credit Risk: Standardised Approach”, open that table in one or two real bank PDFs and see how numbers look.

### 2.5 Buffers & Macro-Prudential Tools

**21. Guidelines for implementation of Countercyclical Capital Buffer (CCCB) in India**

**What:** 2015 RBI circular (DBR.No.BP.BC.71/21.06.201/2014-15 dated 5 Feb 2015) that defines India’s CCyB framework (0–2.5% of RWA based on credit-to-GDP gap).

**Modules:** 3, 11, 12.

**How to use:**

* Read the intro to understand:

  * When CCCB is activated,
  * How the buffer is built up and released, and
  * How it interacts with the **Capital Conservation Buffer (CCB)**.

* Great for explaining macro-prudential capital tools in an India-specific way.

---

### 2.6 Securitisation & Exposures

**22. Reserve Bank of India (Securitisation of Standard Assets) Directions, 2021 (Master Directions)**

**What:** Current Indian securitisation framework for standard assets – true sale conditions, risk retention, credit enhancement, tranching, and derecognition rules.

**Modules:** 8, 10.

**How to use:**

* Combine with Basel securitisation framework (reference 7) to connect **global theory + Indian implementation**.
* Focus on:

  * When banks can derecognise securitised assets, and
  * How credit enhancements and liquidity facilities are treated.

---

### 2.7 IRB / Advanced Approaches in India

**23. Implementation of the Internal Rating Based (IRB) Approach for Credit Risk – RBI Guidelines (2011)**

**What:** RBI guidelines for banks wishing to migrate to IRB (Foundation and Advanced), covering eligibility, data requirements, model governance, and validation.

**Modules:** 12.

**How to use:**

* Focus on:

  * Minimum data history,
  * Default definition and alignment,
  * “Use-test” and governance expectations.

* Very useful to talk about **IRB from an Indian regulator’s perspective**.

---

**24. Implementation of New Capital Adequacy Framework (Basel II) in India – early RBI circulars (2005/2007)**

**What:** Circulars announcing migration to Basel II, defining allowed approaches (SA for credit, BIA/SA for op-risk) and parallel-run requirements.

**Modules:** 2, 4, 12.

**How to use:**

* Good for **historical context** – helps you explain phased implementation and parallel-run concepts in India.
