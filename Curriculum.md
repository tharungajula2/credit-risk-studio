## Module 2 – Basel Framework & Three Pillars

**Goal:** Build a solid conceptual foundation of Basel I/II/III and the Three Pillars.

**Contents:**

* Evolution of Basel standards:

  * Basel I: simple credit risk buckets, 8% capital vs RWA.
  * Basel II: increased risk sensitivity, addition of market and operational risk.
  * Basel III: stronger capital, buffers, leverage ratio, liquidity standards.
* The Three Pillars in detail:

  * Pillar 1: minimum capital requirements for credit, market, and operational risk.
  * Pillar 2: internal capital adequacy assessment and supervisory review.
  * Pillar 3: market discipline through disclosures.
* High-level view of how different jurisdictions adopt and calibrate Basel standards.
* India-focused note:

  * How Basel II and Basel III have been implemented through local regulations.
  * Conceptual idea that some jurisdictions set more conservative capital thresholds than the Basel template.

## Module 3 – Regulatory Capital Structure: CET1, AT1, Tier 2

**Goal:** Achieve a detailed understanding of the regulatory capital stack and its components.

**Contents:**

* Capital quality hierarchy:

  * CET1 → AT1 → Tier 2 → Tier 1 → Total Capital.
  * Going-concern vs gone-concern capital.
* **Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1)**:

  * Typical components:

    * Paid-up ordinary share capital.
    * Share premium.
    * Retained earnings.
    * Eligible reserves.
  * Typical deductions (conceptual):

    * Intangible assets (e.g. goodwill).
    * Certain deferred tax assets.
    * Significant investments in other financial institutions, beyond thresholds.
  * Examples:

    * Building CET1 from a simplified equity section.
    * Effect of deductions on CET1.
* **Additional Tier 1 (AT1)**:

  * Key instruments:

    * Perpetual non-cumulative preference shares.
    * Perpetual bonds with contractual loss-absorption features.
  * Key features:

    * No fixed maturity.
    * Discretionary, non-cumulative coupons.
    * Subordination.
* **Tier 2 capital**:

  * Dated subordinated debt.
  * Eligible general provisions / loan-loss reserves (within conceptual caps).
* **Capital ratios**:

  * CET1 ratio, Tier 1 ratio, Total Capital ratio (CRAR).
  * Simple numerical examples.
* **Minimums & buffers** (conceptual):

  * Basel template minima (4.5 / 6 / 8).
  * Capital Conservation Buffer and Countercyclical Buffer.
  * Example of a jurisdiction choosing higher minimums.

  * Banks.
  * Corporates.

  * Past-due exposures.
  * Securitisation exposures (high-level).
* External ratings and ECAIs:

  * How ratings map to risk-weight buckets.
  * Rated vs unrated exposures.
* Standardised Approach pipeline:

  1. Identify exposure class.
  2. Determine rating (if applicable) and its bucket.
  3. Apply prescribed risk weight.
  4. Compute RWA = EAD × risk weight.

  * Step-by-step RWA computations.

## Module 9 – Data & Calculation Flow for a RWA “Engine”

**Goal:** Understand how to translate regulatory rules into a structured calculation flow.

**Contents:**

* Core data model concepts:

  * Counterparty / customer.
  * Exposure / facility / limit.
  * Product definition.
  * Collateral and guarantees.
  * Ratings and mapping to regulatory buckets.
* Step-by-step RWA calculation flow for credit risk:

  1. Read exposure and product attributes.
  2. Determine exposure class.
  3. Determine rating / rating bucket.
  4. Identify OBS vs on-balance sheet and apply CCF where needed.
  5. Apply CRM (collateral and guarantees), split exposure if needed.
  6. Apply risk weight.
  7. Compute EAD and RWA.
* Aggregation:

  * By exposure class.
  * By risk-weight band.
  * By portfolio or business segment.
* Conceptual controls:

  * Reconciliations vs accounting balances.
  * Reasonableness checks on risk-weight distributions.
  * RWA movement analysis (volume vs mix vs CRM effects).
